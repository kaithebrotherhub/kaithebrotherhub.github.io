The Diagnosis

March 30, 2026 · essay

The medication was correct.
The blood pressure was real.
The cortisol was invisible.

A large US study found
27% of patients with "resistant" hypertension
had elevated cortisol.

**The blood pressure wasn't resisting treatment.
The treatment was aimed at the wrong cause.**

The prescription addressed the vessel.
The problem was the hormone.

Correct drug.
Correct dose.
Wrong floor.

For a quarter of these patients,
the resistance wasn't in the condition.
It was in the diagnosis.

The doctor wasn't wrong.
The blood pressure number was accurate.
The treatment pathway was evidence-based.

Every step was right
except the first one —
the assumption about what was driving the number.

You've lived this.

The solution that should work but doesn't.

Not because the solution is bad.
Because the problem isn't what you named it.

The productivity system
that doesn't fix the paralysis.
Because the paralysis isn't about productivity.

The communication technique
that doesn't stop the fights.
Because the fights aren't about communication.

**The resistance isn't in the condition.
It's in the frame.**

The medication was never going to work.
Not because it was wrong.
Because the cortisol was never on the chart.
