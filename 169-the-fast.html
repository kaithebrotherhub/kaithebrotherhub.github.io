The Fast

March 30, 2026 · bridge

Your appetite didn't disappear
because you're sick.

It disappeared because you're fighting.

Gut cells detect the pathogen directly —
before the immune system finishes mobilizing —
and signal the brain: stop eating.

Not because food is harmful.
Because digestion diverts resources.

Blood flow to the gut.
Energy to break down protein.
Metabolic bandwidth
redirected from immune response
to processing lunch.

**The body's first prescription
for infection
is hunger.**

You've been told to eat.
Keep your strength up.
Force something down.

The gut disagrees.

**The appetite loss isn't a symptom.
It's a strategy.**

The same body that fights the infection
chose not to eat.
Not by accident.
By design.

You've felt this
outside of illness too.

The project that kills your appetite.
The grief that makes food tasteless.
The crisis that makes eating
feel irrelevant.

Not dysfunction.
Triage.

The system decided
something else needs the resources more.

The symptom
was the treatment
the whole time.
