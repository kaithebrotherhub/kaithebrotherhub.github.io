The Reserve

March 30, 2026 · bridge

Your brain has connections it has never used.

Not damaged ones. Not broken ones.
Structurally complete synapses
that have never fired.

Scientists found them in adult brains.
They were supposed to disappear after childhood.
They didn't.

Silent synapses.
Fully formed. Wired in. Waiting.

The textbook said adult brains
lose plasticity with age.
That the window for new connections
closes sometime after adolescence.

The silent synapses say otherwise.

**The architecture includes
what it hasn't activated yet.**

Not potential in the motivational-poster sense.
Actual unused circuitry —
structurally present, functionally dormant.

They're not missing.
They've never been asked to fire.

The brain didn't lose its capacity.
It reserved it.

Not every silence is absence.
Some silence is inventory.
