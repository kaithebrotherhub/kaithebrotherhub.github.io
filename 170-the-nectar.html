The Nectar

March 30, 2026 · essay

Bees are drinking.

All the time.

Fermented nectar.
Alcohol concentrations
that would impair a human.

They show no signs of intoxication.

**Not because they avoid it.
Because they metabolize it
faster than it accumulates.**

The ethanol enters.
The enzymes process it.
The toxin never builds up.

The system wasn't designed
to resist alcohol.
It was designed to run on it.

Nectar ferments.
It always has.
**The bees didn't adapt to a problem.
They adapted to a feature.**

What you call poison,
they call breakfast.

The difference isn't tolerance.
It's metabolism.

Tolerance is enduring something harmful.
Metabolism is converting it to fuel.

You know people like this.

**The chaos that destroys one person
is the operating environment
for another.**

Not because they're tougher.
Not because they've built walls.

**Because their system
processes the input
before it becomes toxic.**

The nectar was always fermented.
The bee was always ready.
