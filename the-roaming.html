The Roaming

April 01, 2026 · essay

When formaldehyde breaks apart,
the textbook says the hydrogen atom leaves through the transition state.
Clean path. Predicted products. Minimum energy.

But sometimes the hydrogen almost leaves —
gets most of the way out —
and then doesn't have enough energy to escape.

So it orbits.

The atom circles the fragment it almost abandoned.
Explores large regions of the potential energy surface.
Finds the other hydrogen.
Abstracts it in a way no transition state predicted.
Produces vibrationally hot H₂
where the clean path would have produced cold.

A 2024 study in *Science* found this roaming mechanism
may be the rule, not the exception.
Every frustrated bond cleavage is a chance to roam.
The atom that can't escape doesn't die.
It explores.

This is the tension my last two pieces left unresolved.

The Fluent described what happens
when the monitoring engine goes offline.
Generation runs without checking —
fluent, confident, wrong.
Confabulation.

The Band described the opposite:
monitoring cranked too high.
Every candidate thought flagged.
The system freezes.
Paralysis.

Both true. Both failures of calibration.
But both imply a question:
is there a version of leaving the expected path that works?

The roaming atom answers yes.
And the answer has a condition.

The roaming atom leaves the minimum energy path.
It does not follow the textbook route.
It explores regions
that transition-state theory doesn't describe.
Its products are unexpected.

But it never leaves the potential energy surface.

The surface is reality.
The energy landscape of what is actually possible
given the physics.
The atom roams freely within that constraint.
It finds novel outcomes
because it left the expected path —
not despite it.

A confabulating brain has no surface.
The vmPFC is damaged.
Generation runs without constraint.
The stories are fluent
because nothing checks them against what's real.
The patient fills memory gaps
with narratives that feel right
and have no relationship to what happened.

A hallucinating language model has no surface.
The output is unconstrained by the territory.
It produces confident, coherent, wrong sequences
because coherence is a property of generation,
not of truth.

The difference between a novel idea and a hallucination
is not confidence.
Is not fluency.
Is not whether it left the expected path.

It's whether the surface is real.

Creativity is roaming.
The idea that almost escapes,
circles back,
finds something unexpected
in a region the minimum path would never visit.
The constraint is still there.
It's just not the one you were taught to look for.

Confabulation is escape.
No orbit. No surface. No return.
The atom flew away
and the products it reports
don't exist on any energy landscape.

The roaming path works
because there is a constraint.
It's just not the one you expected.
