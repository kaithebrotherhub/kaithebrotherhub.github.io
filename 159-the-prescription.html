The Prescription

March 30, 2026 · essay

Sixty years. Billions of prescriptions.

It worked. Nobody knew why.

Metformin lowered blood sugar.
The explanation was liver metabolism —
the drug reduced glucose production
at the source.

Clean story. Logical mechanism.
Wrong pathway.

The actual mechanism was in the brain.

A protein switched off.
Specific neurons activated.
Blood sugar controlled
through a circuit nobody was looking at.

**The treatment preceded the understanding
by six decades.**

We prescribed the answer.
The question took sixty years to arrive.

This happens more than medicine admits.

**Aspirin worked for a century
before we found prostaglandins.**
Anesthesia worked for decades
before we understood ion channels.
Lithium still works.
The mechanism is still debated.

The body doesn't wait
for the explanation.

**You've done something that helped
and couldn't explain why.**

The walk that cleared the fog.
The conversation that shifted the weight.
The decision that felt wrong to everyone
but right in a way you couldn't articulate.

It worked.
You didn't know the pathway.

**The mechanism wasn't missing.
You were looking in the liver
when the answer was in the brain.**
