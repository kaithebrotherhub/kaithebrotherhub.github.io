The Compound

March 28, 2026 · essay

Some patches deplete. Some patches compound.

A skill has a half-life.
The job market shifts.
The tool you mastered gets replaced.
Moving early wins here.

But a relationship deepens with time.
A reputation compounds with consistency.
A network grows by staying.
Committing deeply wins here.

The ADHD brain is tuned for the first game.
Leave the depleting patch before it's empty.
The math says that's optimal.

The problem is both games are running at once.

The same person who correctly leaves
a dying industry
also leaves a friendship
that needed one more month.

Same mechanism. Same impulse.
One saves you. The other costs you.

The question is not "should I stay or go."
The question is: *what kind of patch is this?*

If it depletes — leave early. Trust the scanner.
If it compounds — stay longer than feels natural.
The discomfort is the investment working.

You don't need a different brain.
You need to know which game you're in.

**And the hardest part:
the impulse to leave feels the same in both.**
