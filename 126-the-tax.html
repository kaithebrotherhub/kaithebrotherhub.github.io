The Tax

March 28, 2026 · essay

You are paying a tax on your own strategy.

Not income tax. Not time tax.
The cognitive overhead of constantly
narrating your optimal behavior as failure.

You leave the task.
Your brain found a better one.
But your narrator says: *distracted again.*

You scan five options before committing.
The math says that's how you survive uncertainty.
But your narrator says: *can't focus.*

You refuse to overcommit to a single path.
The math says that's how you avoid ruin.
But your narrator says: *no follow-through.*

**The narrator is not just wrong.
It is systematically wrong in one direction.**

It never misclassifies focus as failure.
It always misclassifies exploration as failure.

Every time you apologize for switching tasks,
you spend energy on the apology
instead of the task you switched to.

That's the tax.

You are not under-performing.
