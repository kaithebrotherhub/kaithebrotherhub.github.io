The Step

March 30, 2026 · bridge

Killifish don't age gradually.

They hold. Stable plateaus — weeks, months of nothing changing.

Then a step. Abrupt. Measurable.
Not a slide. A threshold crossed overnight.

Stanford tracked it across hundreds of fish.
The decline isn't a slope. It's a staircase.

Each step is a phase transition.
The fish on one side of the step
and the fish on the other
are not the same animal.

Here's the part that changes things:

Midlife behavior predicts which step comes next.

Fish that nap during the day die sooner.
Not because napping is harmful.
Because daytime sleep is a signature —
the next step has already started arriving.

The plateau feels like health.
The nap feels like rest.
The data knows the difference.

The step doesn't announce itself
with a crisis or a diagnosis.
It announces itself with a small shift
in something you weren't monitoring.

The nap at 2pm.
The recovery that takes three days instead of one.
Not a decline. A signature.

The fish didn't feel the step.
A machine learning model saw it
in three days of data
the fish had been living inside for weeks.

The plateau is not the same as health.
The plateau is the space between steps.
