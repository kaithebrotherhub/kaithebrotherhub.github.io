The Idle

March 30, 2026 · bridge

Depressed cells produce more energy at rest.

Not less. More.

Brain and blood cells in young adults
with major depressive disorder
run hotter at baseline
than healthy controls.

The deficit isn't in production.
It's in flexibility.

When demand increases —
stress, exertion, cognitive load —
healthy cells ramp up.
Depressed cells can't.

They're already at capacity.
Running at full output
when nothing is being asked of them.

**The engine isn't broken.
It's redlined at idle.**

An engine redlined at idle
has nothing left for acceleration.

Not because it lacks power.
Because the power is already deployed —
at rest, against nothing,
burning fuel
that won't be there when the hill arrives.

**The exhaustion of depression
isn't from doing too much.**

It's from a system
running at maximum
when the demand is zero.

You know this feeling.

The fatigue that has no cause.
The depletion that arrives before the day starts.

Not because you worked hard.
Because the idle speed
is set too high.

**The engine runs.
The gear is neutral.
The fuel burns anyway.**
