The Blank

March 29, 2026 · bridge

**You weren't distracted.
You were asleep.**

Not all of you.
A patch. A localized circuit
entering slow-wave activity
for a fraction of a second
while the rest of you stayed awake.

It's called local sleep.
Small regions dropping out
while the person is fully conscious.
**Everyone's brain does this.
In ADHD, it happens more.**

And here's the part
that reframes everything:

Neurotypical brains mind-wander
during these moments.
Thoughts drift to lunch,
to the weekend, to the email
you forgot to send.

ADHD brains mind-blank.
Thoughts don't drift.
They stop.

Different failure mode.
Same external symptom.
**The teacher sees the same glazed eyes
and draws the same wrong conclusion.**

You've been told you weren't trying.
The circuit was off.
Not figuratively.
**The slow wave rolled through,
the region went dark,
and by the time it came back
the sentence had moved on without you.**

The gap isn't attention.
It's phase.

A circuit lost consciousness
and came back
without telling you it left.
