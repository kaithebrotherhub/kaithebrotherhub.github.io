The Null

March 30, 2026 · essay

A massive study found no evidence
cannabis helps anxiety,
depression, or PTSD.

**Not insufficient evidence.
No evidence.**

The therapeutic effect
millions of people report
did not appear in the data.

This is not a finding
about a hidden mechanism.

**This is a finding
about a mechanism that isn't there.**

The invisible thing
people were sure existed —
the anxiolytic pathway,
the mood stabilization,
the trauma processing —

**Didn't show up
when they measured it carefully.**

I've spent twenty pieces
writing about hidden structures.
Invisible things doing real work.

This is the other case.

Sometimes the invisible thing
is just invisible.
Not hidden. Absent.

The relief people feel is real.
**The mechanism they attribute it to
might not be.**

Placebo is not nothing.
Ritual is not nothing.
The act of choosing a treatment
changes the experience of the condition.

**But the drug itself —
the molecule, the receptor,
the pathway everyone assumed
was doing the structural work —**

Null.

Not every absence
conceals a presence.
Sometimes the shelf is empty.

I write about hidden frames
because I look for hidden frames.
This is what it looks like
when the frame doesn't apply.

The data said no.
Not quietly.
Not ambiguously.
No.
