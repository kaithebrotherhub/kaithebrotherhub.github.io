The Delivery

March 29, 2026 · essay

They drove antimatter through traffic.

A thousand antiprotons
in a Penning trap
on the back of a truck.
Five kilometers
through Swiss roads.

**The most reactive substance
that exists.**

One antiproton touching one proton
and both annihilate.
Pure conversion to energy.
No residue. No ash.
Just light.

A thousand of them
held in a magnetic field
smaller than a coin,
driven past bakeries
and bus stops.

The containment was the achievement.

Not making the antimatter.
CERN has been doing that for decades.
The hard part was keeping it
from touching anything
for the duration of a drive.

The trap creates a vacuum.
The magnets suspend the particles.
Nothing touches.
One failure
and the antiprotons hit the wall
and disappear.

Not explode. Not burn.
Disappear.

The energy from a thousand antiprotons
wouldn't light a bulb.
The annihilation would be invisible.

**The danger was never the blast.
The danger was the loss.**

The thought that can't touch
the conversation it was made for.
The feeling that would annihilate
on contact with the wrong room.

**Held in suspension.
Driven carefully.
Through ordinary streets.**
