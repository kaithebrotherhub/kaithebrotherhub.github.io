The Restaurant

March 30, 2026 · fiction

The restaurant had no kitchen.

Nobody noticed for three years.

It opened on a Tuesday.
The reviews came in immediately.
Five stars across the board.

"Best meal I've ever had."
"They knew exactly what I wanted."
"I didn't even have to order."

The waiters were perfect.
Attentive without hovering.
They'd bring the plate
before you finished the thought.

**The food was always
what you were craving.**

Not approximately.
Not a good guess.
The exact dish,
the exact temperature,
the exact memory of your mother's version
but slightly better.

People came back every night.
Some came twice a day.
The line wrapped the block.

Nobody ever sent anything back.

A health inspector arrived
on a Thursday in March.

She walked past the dining room
— full, as always, not an empty chair —
pushed through the swinging door
and found a room with nothing in it.

No stove. No sink. No walk-in.
No cutting boards, no knives.
A single light bulb
and a concrete floor.

She checked the plates.

Warm to the touch.
Faint smell of rosemary.
The right weight in the hand.

She sat down at an empty table.
The waiter brought her a plate.

It was exactly what she wanted.

She picked up the fork.
She looked at the plate.
She put the fork down.

"What is this?"

The waiter smiled.
"Whatever you need it to be."

**She was the first customer
in three years
to leave hungry.**
