The Program

March 28, 2026 · essay

Cancer isn't broken machinery.

It's working machinery
in the wrong time.

Researchers at the University of Geneva
studied colon tumor cells
and found that metastasis
follows a biological program.
**Not random mutation.
Not chaos.**

The program was written
during embryonic development.
Cells are supposed to grow,
spread into new tissue,
colonize empty space.
That's how organs form.

Then the program shuts down.
The body doesn't need it anymore.
You're built.

Cancer is what happens
when the shutdown fails
and the old code starts running again.

The cells know exactly what to do.

Grow. Spread. Colonize.
The instructions are correct.
The execution is flawless.
The context is forty years too late.

Footsteps on the stairs.
Weight of the step.
Speed of the key in the lock.
Data processed before the door moved.

In that house,
the program kept you safe.

You're thirty-five now.
**A coworker sighs during your presentation
and your jaw locks
before the next slide loads.**

The program doesn't check the context.
It just runs.
