The Sentinel

March 28, 2026 · essay

Pain-sensing neurons don't just report damage.
They maintain the structure around them.

A molecule called RNase4,
produced only by nociceptors —
the cells that make you flinch —
keeps myelin intact.

Myelin is insulation.
The sheath around every nerve fiber
that lets signals travel fast and clean.

Remove the pain neurons,
the insulation degrades.
Not because something attacked it.
Because nothing was maintaining it.

The alarm is load-bearing.

You've tried to silence the scan.
The part that won't stop checking
whether the room is safe,
whether the tone shifted,
whether that pause meant something.

You've named it anxiety.
Built a protocol to manage it.
Told yourself the scan is the problem.

What if the scan is the maintenance?

The grief that won't resolve.
Maybe it's holding a connection open
that nothing else can hold.

The anger you keep trying to breathe through.
Maybe it's maintaining a boundary
your conscious mind won't build.

**You can't always tell which pain is signal
and which pain is infrastructure.**

Silence isn't relief.
Sometimes silence is degradation
you can't feel yet.
