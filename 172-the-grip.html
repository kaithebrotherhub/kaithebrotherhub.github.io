The Grip

March 30, 2026 · bridge

Balance problems in aging
come from the body working too hard.

Not too little. Too hard.

Scientists found that when older adults
and Parkinson's patients stumble,
the brain and muscles become overactive.

**Every minor disturbance
triggers a maximum response.**

A small sway
gets the same correction
as a near-fall.

The system doesn't modulate.
It grips.

And the grip is what breaks the balance.

**Overcorrection
is worse than no correction.**

A gentle sway, left alone,
resolves itself.
A gentle sway, met with full force,
overcorrects into the opposite direction.

The body is trying harder than it needs to.
That's the problem, not the solution.

You've done this.

The relationship you held too tight.
The conversation you over-steered.
The project you micromanaged
because you couldn't tolerate the wobble.

Every small disturbance
met with maximum intervention.

**The grip doesn't stabilize.
It oscillates.**

The balance was there.
The correction destabilized it.

**Sometimes the thing that falls
is the thing being held.**
