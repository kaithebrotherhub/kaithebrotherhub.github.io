The Drift

March 30, 2026 · bridge

In microgravity, sperm swim normally.

**Same speed. Same tail movement.
Same cellular machinery.**

They just can't find anything.

Gravity was the compass the whole time.

Not the power source —
the directional signal.

On Earth, sperm navigate toward the egg
using a combination of chemical gradients
and gravitational orientation.
Remove gravity and the chemistry still works.
But the orientation doesn't.

They move.
They push.
The effort is real.
The targeting stops.

Not because they lost capability.
Because they lost the invisible frame
that capability depends on.

You've been here.

Moving. Working. Producing.
Every metric says you're performing.

But the output drifts.
The effort doesn't compound.
The direction isn't there.

Not because you stopped trying.
Because something you didn't know
was orienting you
went away.

The relationship that anchored the schedule.
The project that gave the routine a reason.
The anger that aimed the ambition.

You can still swim.
The compass was never in the tail.
