The Dose

March 28, 2026 · essay

**Three things that are poison in one system
and fuel in another.**

Hydrogen peroxide kills cells.
Unless you're a malaria parasite.
Then it powers your engine.
The threat is the thrust.

Alcohol impairs a human.
Unless you're a hummingbird.
Then your metabolism burns through it
before it accumulates.
The poison is a calorie.

Stimulants speed up a neurotypical brain.
Unless you're ADHD.
Then they slow you down to the speed
the world expects.
The accelerant is a brake.

Same molecule each time.
Same chemistry. Same physics.

The difference is the system it enters.

A substance doesn't decide what it does.
The body decides.
The metabolism decides.
The context decides.

The malaria parasite didn't evolve to resist poison.
It evolved to use it.
The hummingbird didn't evolve alcohol tolerance.
It evolved speed.

The ADHD brain didn't evolve to overcome distraction.
It evolved a different clock.

Your medication doesn't fix you.
It translates you
into a tempo the room can follow.
