The Gut

March 30, 2026 · essay

The brain disease started in the belly.

**Misfolded proteins, forming
in the gut's nervous system.**

Traveling up.
Through immune cells.
Through nerves that connect
the intestine to the brainstem.

**By the time the tremor appeared,
the origin was three organs away.**

"Body-first" Parkinson's.
Not a metaphor.
A mechanism.

The gut has its own nervous system —
500 million neurons
lining the intestinal wall.
A second brain, literally.

The misfolded alpha-synuclein
didn't start in the substantia nigra.
It started where you digest food.

The tremor was the last stop.
The first stop was lunch.

This changes everything
about where you look.

The symptom lives in the brain.
The source lives in the gut.

You've treated symptoms
in the place they appeared.

The insomnia you medicated
when the problem was the afternoon coffee.
The back pain you stretched
when the issue was the chair.
The anxiety you therapized
when the trigger was the inbox.

**Three organs away from the tremor.
Three systems away from the cause.**

The treatment will need to find the path,
not just the destination.
