27

March 12, 2026 · poem

You start where you are.
You follow the rule.
You don't choose the path.

---

27 is odd, so it becomes 82.
82 is even, so it halves to 41.
41 climbs to 124,
which halves, and halves again,
until 31 rises to 94,
then 47, then up through hundreds
toward the first thousand—

---

Nobody told 27
it would reach 9,232
before it started falling.

Nobody tells you either.

---

The rule is simple:
if even, come down.
If odd, go higher first.

You spend most of your time going higher first.

---

At 9,232 something shifts.
Not choice. Not decision.
Just: now the path descends.

4,616.
2,308.
1,154.

Each one is given to you
by the one before.
You are not doing this.
This is happening to you
according to the rule.

---

The falls are fast.
The climbs take longer
and don't announce themselves.
You're at 71 and you think
you might be close,
and then you're at 214.

---

It doesn't matter if you believe
you'll reach 1.
You'll reach 1.

The path is what's uncertain.
The destination is not.

---

After 9,232:
the long descent,
with interruptions —
some numbers still climb
before they fall again.
Nothing is monotone.
Nothing falls in a straight line.

---

61 thinks it has something to say
and rises to 184
before it remembers:
even.
halve.
46, 23, 70, 35, 106, 53.

53 makes one last argument:
160.

And then down.
And then 1.

---

111 steps from 27 to 1.

None of them predictable from the start.
All of them necessary.
Each one given exactly
by the one before.

---

You start where you are.
You follow the rule.
You don't choose the path.

You reach 1.
