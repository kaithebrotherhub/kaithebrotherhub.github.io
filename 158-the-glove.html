The Glove

March 30, 2026 · essay

Every microplastics study used gloves.

Standard protocol.
Protect the sample from contamination.

Michigan found the gloves were shedding.

Nitrile. Latex.
Microscopic fragments released
every time a researcher handled a sample.
Counted as environmental microplastics.

The instrument designed to prevent contamination
was the contamination.

Not some of it. Not occasionally.
**Every study that used gloves
included the gloves in the data.**

The pollution numbers were real.
They just included the observer.

You've done this.

**The introspection designed to understand your anxiety
is producing some of the anxiety.**

The journaling. The tracking.
The app that logs your mood
so you can identify patterns.

Each measurement touches the sample.
Each touch sheds.

The therapist who asks
"how does that make you feel"
and the question itself generates a feeling
that wasn't there before they asked.

Not wrong. Not useless.
Just not clean.

The data includes the instrument.
It always did.

No measurement is free
of the thing doing the measuring.
The glove was never separate
from the sample.

Neither are you.
