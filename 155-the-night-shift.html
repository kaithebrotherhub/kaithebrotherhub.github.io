The Night Shift

March 30, 2026 · bridge

The fix for the daytime problem happens at night.

**ADHD brains steal slow waves from sleep
and play them during the day.**

Those moments of blank — the circuit going dark
mid-sentence, mid-task, mid-thought —
are sleep-like waves intruding where they don't belong.

The treatment doesn't target the blank.
It targets the night before.

Auditory stimulation during sleep
boosts slow-wave activity where it belongs.
Not drugs. Sound.
Timed pulses that deepen the waves
so the brain gets enough of them at night.

Enough at night means fewer stolen during the day.

The attention problem isn't in the moment you lose focus.
It's in the sleep architecture from the night before.

A factory with a night shift that keeps breaking down
starts pulling day workers onto overnight.
The day shift thins.
Productivity drops.
The manager blames the day workers.

**Fix the night shift.
The day shift fills itself.**

The brain doesn't have an attention deficit.
It has a sleep-distribution problem.

The slow waves aren't missing.
They're in the wrong shift.
