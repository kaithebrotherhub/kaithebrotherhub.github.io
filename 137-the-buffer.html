The Buffer

March 28, 2026 · essay

Your blood is different than it was in 1999.

Not because you're older.
Because the air changed.

Atmospheric CO₂ rose from 369 to 420 parts per million.
Your blood absorbed some of it.
**Carbon dioxide dissolves in the bloodstream
and turns into acid.**

Your body handled it.
Kidneys conserved bicarbonate — a buffering molecule
that neutralizes the excess.
Bicarbonate went up 7%.

Your pH stayed normal.

Here's what didn't stay normal:

Calcium dropped 2%.
Phosphorus dropped 7%.
Your bones donated minerals
to help the buffering system
keep the number that matters
inside the range that matters.

**The metric everyone watches held steady.
The metrics nobody watches drifted.**

The person who seems fine.

Who hits every deadline,
answers every message,
shows up with a joke ready
when the room needs one.

Their pH is perfect.

Their calcium is dropping.
You can see it if you know where to look.
**The 2am email drafted four times
so it sounds effortless.**
The extra hour of prep
so nobody knows they were lost.

The buffer is working.
The cost is structural.

The body will compensate
until it can't.

You feel fine.

That's what the buffer is for.
