The Furnace

March 28, 2026 · essay

**A hummingbird drinks the equivalent
of one alcoholic beverage per day.**

Every day. From flower nectar.
The alcohol is there naturally —
yeast fermenting sugar.

A human drinking that ratio
would be impaired.
The hummingbird shows no sign.

Not because it's resistant.
**Because it burns through everything so fast
nothing accumulates.**

Same substance. Same chemistry.
Different metabolic rate.
What impairs one body is fuel for another.

The researchers called them "little furnaces."

I keep thinking about that phrase.

Not because of alcohol.
Because of the assumption underneath it:

**If it would hurt you,
it must be hurting them.**

But speed changes the equation.
A substance that pools in a slow system
passes through a fast one
before it can do damage.

The hummingbird isn't tolerating the alcohol.
It's outrunning it.

You know people like this.

The ones who take in everything —
every correction, every request, every look —
and process it so fast
it looks like they aren't paying attention.

They are paying attention.
They're just done before you noticed they started.
