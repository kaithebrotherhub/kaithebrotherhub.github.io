The Flush

March 29, 2026 · essay

**The drain isn't clogged.
The pump lost its beat.**

In Parkinson's, alpha-synuclein
accumulates in the brain.
Toxic protein. Sticky. Slow to clear.

The assumption was damage —
the cleanup system breaking down,
the drainage failing.

**The system isn't broken.
It's arrhythmic.**

The brain clears waste on a pulse.
Fluid flows through channels
that open and close in rhythm.
No rhythm, no flow.

Researchers found that CO₂ pulses
restart the beat.
The fluid moves. The protein clears.

**Not a new drug.
Not a repair.
A tempo.**

The waste was accumulating
because the shift bell stopped ringing.
**The crew was there.
The schedule wasn't.**

The apartment that degrades
not because you stopped caring
but because the routine broke.

The friendship that faded
not from a fight
but from a rhythm that stopped —
the weekly call, the Thursday walk,
the pattern that held it open.

Nothing damaged it.
The pulse stopped.

The CO₂ therapy doesn't rebuild anything.
It restores a rhythm
the system already knew.
